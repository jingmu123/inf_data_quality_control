It is crucial to correctly identify occupational asthma because the key to successful treatment is prompt removal of the patient from the workplace exposure that is causing the asthma. If occupational asthma is properly identified in a timely manner, and if the exposure to the offending agent ceases within a certain period of time, then the prognosis is favorable. If exposure continues, however, there is a point of "no return" past which a person with occupational asthma may have permanent respiratory symptoms and disability, even if the exposure ceases at a later date.

【1】Occupational asthma is different from work-aggravated asthma. Work-aggravated asthma refers to preexisting asthma that worsens at work. For example, a person with a history of easily controlled asthma in adolescence might develop an increase in asthma symptoms as an adult when working out删除16:<u>¬ </u>doors during cold winter months.
