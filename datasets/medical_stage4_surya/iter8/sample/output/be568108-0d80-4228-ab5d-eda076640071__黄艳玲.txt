【3】Endotracheal intubation: Refers to the procedure in which a specialized breathing tube is placed into the trachea through the mouth or the nasal passages to allow airway support and the administration of extra oxygen.
Endotracheal intubation can be life saving in cases of respiratory failure. It is also performed for major surgery, typically in the operating room when a person is to receive general anesthesia.
Eosiniphilic pneumonia: a rare type of lung disease characterized by breathlessness, and elevated eosinophil counts like asthma, but with abnormal X-ray studies, unlike asthma.
